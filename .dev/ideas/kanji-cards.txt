A physical knowledge-card system for Japanese learning where each card is a minimal, single-sided token displaying only the core prompt (kanji, word, sentence, grammar pattern, or concept question) plus a small hex ID and optional QR code. The card acts as a physical key: you pick it up, attempt recall from memory, then scan the ID with a companion app (React/TS, C#/ASP.NET, Tailwind) to see the full digital content—meanings, readings, breakdowns, links, AI queries. Cards are printed 8-up on A4 sheets with dynamic font sizing (algorithmic default plus a manual slider override for aesthetic control) and stored in boxes or binders, growing over time into a tangible, collectible knowledge library that bridges the satisfaction of physical ownership with the power of a searchable, updatable digital brain.
