Unified Content Tracker (Anime, Books, Courses, Podcasts, etc.)

Problem: You consume many types of content (anime, novels, video courses, podcasts) for different purposes – fun, learning, immersion. Currently scattered, no unified progress view, no privacy controls, no balanced diet nudges.

Dream: A single system where every content item (anime, book, course, podcast) is tracked with:
- Multiple lists: Want to Start, In Progress, Completed, On Hold, Dropped.
- Titles in English, Japanese, original.
- Cover art, genres, tags, source links.
- Total length (episodes/pages/minutes).
- Multiple purpose-specific progress bars: For Fun, For Learning, For Immersion (i+1 input). Each has its own status and progress counter (e.g., episode watched for fun vs. watched for immersion).
- Privacy toggle (🙈) to hide sensitive titles from others.
- Notes, quotes, actionables per content and per purpose.
- Balanced diet nudges: gentle warnings if you're over-consuming one genre.
- Author/creator profiles.
- Universal search, hearts, bookmarks, tags.
- Works for courses: lectures become "episodes", progress tracked per lecture, notes per section.

MVP: A single list "Watching/Reading" with items having title, privacy toggle, total episodes/pages, and two simple progress bars (fun and learning). No complex recommendations yet. Data in localStorage.
